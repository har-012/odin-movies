Biryani


Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani - Swasthi's Recipes Biryani is a fragrant, mixed rice dish from South Asia, layering aromatic spices, long-grain rice (often basmati), and marinated meat (chicken, lamb, goat), seafood, or vegetables, creating a rich, flavorful main course with numerous regional variations, often slow-cooked in sealed pots for deep flavor infusion. Its name comes from the Persian word for "fried" or "roasted," and it's known for its complex flavors from ingredients like saffron, yogurt, mint, and caramelized onions. \

Ingredients

  • Basmati Rice
  • Chicken
  • Marinada
  • Aromatics
  • Whole spices
  • Ghee
  • Fried Onions

Making

  1. Preparation

    2. Marinate Chicken: Cut chicken, mix with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices (turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, etc.), and herbs; marinate for a few hours or overnight. Soak Rice: Wash and soak basmati rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain. Prepare Saffron Milk: Soak saffron strands in warm milk. Fry Onions: Fry sliced onions in ghee or oil until golden brown, removing half for garnish.


  2. Cook Rice

    3. Boil water with whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cumin) and salt. Add drained rice and cook until it's about 70-90% cooked (al dente, not mushy). Drain immediately


  3. Cook Chicken

    4. In a heavy pot, saut√© ginger-garlic paste and whole spices. Add marinated chicken and cook until partially done


  4. Layer the Biryani (Dum)

    5. Spread the chicken mixture at the bottom of the pot. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken. Sprinkle with fried onions, chopped mint, and coriander leaves. Drizzle with saffron milk and ghee. Seal the pot tightly with foil and a heavy lid to trap steam (dum)


  5. Cook & Rest

    6. Cook on very low heat (stove or oven) for 20-25 minutes. Turn off heat and let it rest, covered, for 10-15 minutes


  6. Serve

    7. Gently fluff with a fork from the sides before serving to mix layers. Garnish with more fried onions and serve hot with raita or salad
