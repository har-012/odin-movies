Pulihora


Pulihora (or Pulihora, Pulihora, Puliyogare) is a tangy, spicy, and aromatic South Indian rice dish made with cooked rice, tamarind (or lemon/kokum), curry leaves, peanuts, lentils (urad dal, chana dal), chilies, turmeric, and other spices, often prepared as a temple offering (prasadam) or for festivals, known for its distinctive flavor from a special tempering and spice powder. It's popular in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana , Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Kerala, with variations like Pulihora gojju (paste) or Pulihora podi (powder) made ahead for quick preparation

Ingredients

  • Mustard Seeds
  • Urad Dal (Black Gram)
  • Chana Dal (Bengal Gram)
  • Peanuts
  • Green Chilies (slit)
  • Dry Red Chilies
  • Curry Leaves
  • Asafoetida (Hing)
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Salt (to taste)

Making

  1. Cook the Rice:

    2. Cook rice (like Sona Masoori) with a pinch of turmeric, salt, and a drop of oil, ensuring grains stay separate (not mushy). Spread it on a plate to cool completely.


  2. Extract Tamarind Pulp:

    3. Soak tamarind in hot water, then squeeze and strain to get a thick pulp.


  3. Prepare the Tempering (Popu):

    4. Heat oil and fry mustard seeds, peanuts, chana dal, urad dal until golden and fragrant. Add curry leaves, green chilies, and dry red chilies.


  4. Make the Paste (Goju):

    5. Add the tamarind pulp, turmeric powder, salt, and a bit of jaggery (optional) to the tempering. Cook on low heat until the mixture thickens and oil separates, forming a pulpy paste.


  5. Combine:

    6. Add the cooled rice to the thickened tamarind paste (or vice versa) and mix gently so rice grains don't break


  6. Final Touch (Optional):

    7. Some recipes add a final seasoning of mustard seeds, peanuts, and curry leaves fried in oil, mixed into the rice
