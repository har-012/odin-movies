Butter Chicken


Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani) is a popular Indian curry featuring marinated chicken in a rich, creamy, spiced tomato-based sauce, known for its mild, buttery, and slightly sweet flavor profile, often made with yogurt-marinated chicken cooked then simmered in a smooth sauce of tomatoes, butter, cream, and aromatics like ginger, garlic, and fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi), typically served with naan or rice

Ingredients

  • For the Chicken & Marinade
    • Chicken (boneless, skinless thighs or breasts)
    • Yogurt (plain, full-fat)
    • Ginger & Garlic (fresh, grated/paste)
    • Lemon Juice
    • Spices: Garam Masala, Cumin, Turmeric, Kashmiri Red Chili Powder (for color)
  • For the Sauce (Makhani Gravy):
    • Butter & Oil
    • Onion & Tomatoes (puree or paste)
    • Ginger & Garlic
    • Spices: Garam Masala, Cumin, Coriander, Kashmiri Chili Powder, sometimes whole spices (cardamom, cinnamon, cloves)
      • `
    • Heavy Cream
    • Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves) - essential for authentic flavor
    • Sugar or Honey (to balance tanginess)
    • Salt, to taste

Making

  1. Marinate the Chicken
    1. Cut boneless chicken into bite-sized pieces.

    2. Combine with yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, Kashmiri red chili powder, salt, and garam masala.

    3. Mix well to coat, then refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or ideally several hours or overnight for deeper flavor.

  2. Make the Gravy Base
    1. Saut√© onions (optional), ginger, garlic, and whole spices in oil/butter until soft.

    2. Add chopped tomatoes, cashews (soaked if possible), and spices (chili, cumin, coriander).

    3. Cook until tomatoes are soft and mushy, adding a little water if needed.

    4. Cool the mixture, then blend with water to a very smooth, fine puree. Strain for extra smoothness if desired

  3. Cook the Chicken & Finish
    1. Cook the marinated chicken pieces in a hot pan until browned and mostly cooked (pan-fry, grill, or air-fry). Set aside.

    2. Heat butter in the same pan, add the smooth gravy puree, and cook on low heat until the oil separates.

    3. Add the cooked chicken to the gravy.

    4. Stir in fresh cream, crushed kasuri methi, and a touch more butter/color if desired.

    5. Simmer gently for 5-10 minutes until flavors meld and the sauce thickens. Garnish with cilantro and serve with naan or rice.
